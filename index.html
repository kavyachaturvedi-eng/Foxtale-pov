Failure 1 — No accountability before forecast changes land

When the growth team revises a demand figure — even a 25% reduction on a high-volume SKU — there is no system check, no justification requirement, and no approval gate. By the time anyone notices, the manufacturer has already begun production based on the previous number. The accountability mechanism (an email from Operations) fires after the commitment, not before it.

The real cost
Every unjustified demand reduction after a manufacturer commitment represents either wasted procurement spend or a renegotiation penalty. The supply chain team estimates this happens multiple times per month across SKUs.
Failure 2 — 10% of orders are M1 emergency purchase orders

In a business shipping approximately 30 lakh units per month, 10% as M1 means 3 lakh units per month procured in emergency mode. Both Sunscreen Matte (43,167 units) and Sunscreen Dewy (54,687 units) are M1 POs in the February 2026 cycle — the two highest-volume SKUs — going to the same manufacturer simultaneously.

Failure 3 — Simultaneous stockouts and dead stock in the same cycle

Ceramide Moisturizer is 18 days from running out. Sunscreen Matte is at 67 days of stock — 22 days above the target. Both conditions exist at the same time, in the same company, in the same month. This is not bad luck. It is the direct output of a forecasting process with no feedback mechanism and no system-enforced accuracy standard.

Failure 4 — Silent formula corruption in every PO calculation

The PO calculation depends on four inputs. Closing inventory is the only one that auto-syncs from Unicommerce. Open PO, GRN Pending, and In-Transit are manually entered by the supply chain team into Excel every cycle. Any transcription error propagates silently through the entire M1, M2, and M3 calculation. The formula produces a wrong answer that looks exactly like a right answer.

Highest technical risk
Every inventory forecasting platform operating at this scale auto-syncs these three data points from the WMS. Running them manually is a structural accuracy risk — independent of how well the demand forecast is constructed.
Failure 5 — Fear buffer contagion after every stockout

When one SKU runs out, the team adds safety buffer to every SKU in the catalogue — not just the one that stocked out. After each stockout event, company-wide Days on Hand increases by approximately 7 to 10 days. Working capital is locked unnecessarily across products that had no supply risk.

Failure 6 — Every decision's reasoning is permanently lost

When a forecast is revised, when a buffer is added, when a demand target changes — the reasoning is never recorded. Six months later, no one can answer why a particular choice was made. V18, V19, V20 are the entire audit trail. The institutional knowledge of the business lives in people's memories and chat histories, not in a system that can be reviewed, improved, or held accountable.

"The problem is not that the team is making bad decisions. The problem is that the system makes it impossible to make consistently good ones."

Part 02

The situation

The business mechanics that make this problem expensive — and the constraints any solution must respect. A replacement system must replicate this logic exactly or the formula outputs will be wrong.

2.1Secondary demand vs primary demand
Secondary demandWhat end customers buy from the marketplace or website. The growth team's monthly target. Measured in units sold by the channel.
Primary demandWhat Foxtale must procure from manufacturers and hold in its warehouse. Always calculated from secondary — never set independently. Always larger than secondary for marketplace channels.
The 45-day ruleFoxtale's warehouse should always hold 45 days of upcoming secondary demand in stock. Manufacturing + shipping = ~30 days. Safety buffer = 15 days. Total = 45-day target DOH.
When Primary = SecondaryWebsite (D2C) and Offline/GT only. Foxtale ships directly — no fulfilment centre buffer layer. No speculative stocking for GT to avoid RTV risk.
2.2The definitive formulas
PRIMARY DEMAND — MARKETPLACE CHANNELS (10 CHANNELS)

Target Closing = 45 days of projected secondary demand

Primary Demand = Target Closing
                   - Closing Inventory ← auto-pulled from Unicommerce daily
                   - In-Transit Stock ← MP In-Transit + WH In-Transit
                   - GRN Pending ← dispatched by mfr, not yet received
                   - Open PO ← raised but not yet dispatched
                   + M0 Actual ← current month's actual sales to date
PRIMARY DEMAND — WEBSITE AND OFFLINE (2 CHANNELS)

Primary Demand = Secondary Demand
No FC buffer layer. Foxtale ships direct. No speculative stocking for GT.
PO CALCULATION — M1, M2, M3

base = Target Closing - Closing Inv - In-Transit - GRN Pending - Open PO
         + Consumption from SNOP date to PO raise date

M1 PO = max(0, base) ← should be zero. Non-zero = planning failure.
M2 PO = max(0, base - M1 PO)
M3 PO = max(0, base - M1 PO - M2 PO)

Current M1 rate: ~10% Target: under 2%

3.2The seven features
01  ·  Foundation + ML
Intelligent demand input with model-generated baseline
The growth team no longer opens a blank form. They open a pre-filled table. Model-generated M1 to M4 forecasts for every SKU and channel. Three model tracks: stable SKUs (AZ/BZ, ~85% auto), volatile SKUs (AX/BX/AY, ~65% model + human), new launches (CX, ~10% analogue baseline). Confidence shown per cell. Human accepts, overrides with reason tag, or adds context. Every override becomes training data.
Time per cycle: 2-3 days today — 3-4 hours after go-live
02  ·  Accountability
Pre-submission validation gates A and B
Gate A: system compares current entry to M-1, M-2, M-3 prior submissions. If |variance %| exceeds threshold, Submit is disabled. Justification text (min 20 chars) + reason tag (Competitor launch / Sale event / Campaign change / MRP change / Other) both required before re-enabling. AX SKUs: 10% threshold. All others: 20%. Configurable by analytics without engineering. Gate B: AOP alignment check. Warning, not a block.
First gate in Foxtale history that fires before the manufacturer is impacted
03  ·  Foundation
Primary demand and M1/M2/M3 PO auto-calculation
Once demand is submitted and validated, the platform computes primary demand and M1, M2, M3 PO quantities automatically for every SKU and manufacturer. All four inventory inputs pulled from Unicommerce in real time. M1 POs flagged as a planning failure signal. PO output grouped by manufacturer, formatted for Unicommerce upload.
M1 PO rate from ~10% to under 2% within 6 months of go-live
04  ·  Governance
5-stage maker-checker approval flow
System computes — SCM reviews computed POs against capacity and budget — Analytics monitors and flags deviations — SCM approves with one click (approver logged with timestamp) — Unicommerce auto-raises PO and notifies manufacturer. Approver cannot be the submitter. Slack notification, 24-hour SLA, automatic escalation if not actioned. Full permanent audit log, 24-month retention.
Every decision auditable in 3 seconds. "Who approved this?" has an answer.
05  ·  Intelligence
M-1 / M-2 / M-3 variance tracking with MAPE
For every SKU and channel, shows how the forecast for each month evolved across submission cycles. Reveals systematic drift patterns — like Sunscreen Matte declining 4 consecutive cycles without detection. MAPE score per channel team updated monthly. Teams with lower accuracy face tighter gate thresholds automatically. Pattern alert: same SKU drifts more than 10% for 3+ consecutive cycles.
Forecasting quality becomes a measurable, improvable metric
06  ·  Intelligence
ABC x XYZ SKU classification matrix
Every SKU auto-classified into one of nine buckets by revenue contribution (ABC) and forecast variance (XYZ). AX SKUs — highest revenue, most volatile — receive weekly review and 10% threshold. CZ SKUs receive quarterly oversight and 25% threshold. Classification auto-computed monthly from actual sales and variance data. No manual tagging.
The right level of attention on the right products, automatically
07  ·  Intelligence
External context capture per submission
Structured context tags per submission — Sale Event (Big Billion Days), Campaign Drop, MRP Change, New Listing, Influencer Push. This contextual data currently lives only in WhatsApp messages. Capturing it systematically is what makes the model learn from events, not just from numbers. Tags stored permanently with the demand record and fed into monthly model retrain.

Part 04

How it works

A single demand cycle from the moment the growth team opens the platform to the moment a purchase order lands at the manufacturer. This is the full user journey after Phase 1 is live.

4.1The demand cycle — step by step
01
Growth team opens the platform on the first working day of the demand window
The platform shows each channel's demand input screen with baseline figures pre-populated from the previous 3 months of actuals. For each SKU across M1 to M4, the team reviews the baseline and adjusts for known factors.
02
Pre-submission checks run in real time as they type
If a change exceeds the SKU's variance threshold, the Submit button is disabled and a justification drawer appears. The submitter cannot proceed without a written reason and a reason tag. Both stored permanently.
03
AOP alignment check fires on the full submission
Total demand GMV for the month compared to the Annual Operating Plan. If material deviation, Analytics team notified. Warning gate — does not block submission.
04
Submission locked and versioned
System immediately computes primary demand for every SKU and channel, pulling inventory data from Unicommerce in real time. Any revision creates a new version and re-runs all checks.
05
M1, M2, M3 PO quantities calculated per SKU per manufacturer
M1 POs flagged as planning failure signal. Any PO that deviates significantly from the prior cycle flagged for Analytics review. Output grouped by manufacturer.
06
SCM team receives the PO quantities for review and approval
Interface shows old quantity, new quantity, variance from prior cycle, and the growth team's justification if one was required. Approver sees all context needed to decide in 30 seconds.
07
SCM approves with one click — logged with timestamp
Approver identity and timestamp stored permanently. Approver cannot be the submitter. Analytics team can see the full log at any time.
08
Approved POs auto-raised on Unicommerce — manufacturer notified
No manual re-entry. No email required. The SCM team does not touch a spreadsheet.
4.2Monday morning — before and after
Today
6 hours building the model
Compiling channel files. Running Excel formulas. Chasing missing data. Manually entering Open PO, GRN, and In-Transit figures. Recalculating after late submissions. Emailing PO numbers to manufacturers. The team's intelligence is consumed by making the spreadsheet correct, not by making good decisions.
After Phase 1
30 minutes reviewing exceptions
The platform ran overnight. The team opens the dashboard and sees: which SKUs breached DOH thresholds, which forecast changes need approval, which M1 POs are forming. They review, approve where appropriate, escalate where needed. The calculation is done. The decision is theirs.

5.2Open queries — must confirm before coding begins

These are factual questions about business rules that only Foxtale can answer. No assumptions will be made. Every item below must be resolved before Week 3.

Q1
Demand cycle dates
What is the fixed open and close date each month? Can demand be revised after submission within the same cycle, and if so, how many revisions are allowed?
Q2
Variance threshold
Is the alert threshold a single global figure (20%), or does it vary by ABC/XYZ tier? Is the AX threshold confirmed as 10%?
Q3
Approval chain
Who is notified first — growth team or analytics? Who has authority to approve a demand change? What is the expected SLA in hours? Is Slack the agreed approval surface?
Q4
45-day rule flexibility
Is the 45-day buffer fixed for all SKUs, or does it vary — e.g., sunscreen higher in March to May, quick commerce channels lower due to faster replenishment cycles?
Q5
B2B outright channels
Are Nykaa B2B, Purplle, and Tira treated identically to B2C channels for primary demand derivation, or does the outright model change the formula?
Q6
SNOP date
Is the S&OP review meeting on a fixed day each month? Who sets it? This is a critical variable in the M1 formula — determines consumption from SNOP to PO raise date.
Q7
Manufacturer lead times
Is lead time currently tracked per manufacturer or not at all? A 60-day lead time effectively makes every M3 PO an M1. This determines which PO bucket each demand signal falls into.
Q8
Offline/GT submission
Is Offline/GT demand submitted by the same growth team or by a separate sales team on a different cadence?
Q9
Marketing spend granularity
Is daily or weekly spend data available from Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, Amazon Ads Console? Monthly totals exist in Excel but daily/weekly granularity is needed for the model to learn demand-lag from spend spikes.
Q10
Sale event calendar
Does a record exist of which months had major platform sale events (Amazon GIF, Flipkart BBD, Nykaa Pink Friday, Republic Day) and the approximate demand lift per channel? Without this, the model treats sale-event months as unexplained outliers and will systematically under-forecast every sale month.
